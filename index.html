Project Idea

Overview

The aim of this project is to create a game in unity that incorporates elements from the pokemon game franchise and turns them into a product that challenges, entertains and is aimed at an adult audience. There is room in gamer’s lives for an update to the pokemon formula and it is not currently being fulfilled by anyone, not even pokemon’s developers. The core components of tactical battling, catching, raising and training of fantastical pets are enthralling; and I am certain it will be a great joy to bring these exciting and satisfying elements back to the forefront, updated and better than ever.

Motivation

Gamers the world over, especially players of pokemon, have consistently expressed weariness towards GameFreak's inability to improve the pokemon formula. They want so badly to play and enjoy a pokemon game, but are found wanting when they realise that they are still being designed to appeal to children. The appealing aspects of pokemon are also entertaining to adults, but GameFreak refuses to capitalise on this and is stick with a decades old formula they refuse to deviate from. Developing a good version of pokemon provides on opportunity to not be constrained by gamefreaks desire to make money over art, and create something truly fun and engaging for an audience that is not being capitalised on.

Description

The game will have similar fundamentals to any pokemon game. An overworld will be available to explore which will be utilised to provide a place to obtain monsters for the player's collection/team. In the overworld segment of the game, the player will control their character from a top down isometric perspective and will control their character primarily using the WASD keys. While walking around in the overworld, the player will be able to interact and examine objects and people; this is how they will be able to discover the secrets of the lands they will be travelling to. These lands, which will be varied in environment and foliage, will contain the monsters that can be tamed and battled. They will be encountered semi randomly; they will appear in the environment of the overworld as tiny sprites and when run into will trigger an encounter with the player. Some monsters will not be able to be obtained without triggering encounters with other monsters, to give players a reason to revisit old areas and grind encounters to look for a desirable monster. Players will form parties of six and do battle with wild monsters and non player characters. Strategic battle will play out in turn based combat on a grid made up of randomly generated terrain. Each monster will have unique strengths and weaknesses, including but not limited to movements speed, movement priority, range, physical power, magical power, area of effect and reflexes. Each monster will have unique abilities, powers and combat strategies which will make assembling a team and a gameplan engaging. Aesthetically, each monster will have a standout look to be visually appealing and to immerse the player into the game. The whole game will have a realistic and wild look to it, to emphasise the fact that monsters and human must survive in this world. Not all monsters are large and hulking however. Some are small and tricksy, others nimble and timid. The jungles will be menacing and looming, the swamps putrid and unforgiving, the deserts vast and treacherous and any human civilisation forms magical bastions against the grand creatures of this world. Shamans, wizards and friendly neighborhood oracles are insurance against the mighty outside beasts, with deep trenches and artillery being useful physical deterrents against the larger roaming monsters. The player character is not magically or scientifically gifted however – rather they have an understanding of the bond that all things in this world share and the equal role that monsters play in the makeup of the universe. Using hired dimensional doors, the player can keep their creatures in a plethora of habitats, teach them useful and interesting moves and abilities and bring them along on the adventure of a lifetime. The story will answer the question of where monsters and humans came from and what happened before the beginning of the universe. It will also test the player character by putting them up against unsurpassed evil and seeing whether their resolve and morals will hold to the last breath. When put up against power unimagined with no sight of victory, how will the mortal and finite player character respond?

Outcome

Once the game is in a partial state of completion, it would be feasible to show it off to the public. An early build of the game release would be excellent marketing and would generate the beginnings of a community, a community being one of the most powerful tools in fostering a fanbase. Making back the time and money put into the project is an important part of any venture and getting the game exposure will be a big component of that. The final product should be a big hit with the older crowd that used to like pokemon as children and with fans of good video games anywhere.
